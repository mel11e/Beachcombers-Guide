Brazoria National Wildlife Refuge

Witness the sound of over 40,000 snow geese lifting off the ground at this national refugee, or if you're more of a reptilatian admirer come see 12 foot alligators lounging around the banks of this wildlife refuge. Making a trip here only to see the scenery is worth the trip.

Galveston Island State Park

This state park is located on the west end of Galveston Island and covers over 2,000 acres which was purchased by private owers in 1969 by a State Parks Bond Program. A vast variety of nature can be found here from marsh rabbits to sea shore birds.

Learn More
Padre Island National Seashore

Padre Island National Seashore is over 130,000 acres and is the only lengthy barrier island in the world that remains undeveloped. Here you will find numerous diverse plants and flowers, and fun to break up your time here on the island.
